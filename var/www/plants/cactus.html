Cactus - The Desert Survivor

Cacti are remarkable succulent plants belonging to the family Cactaceae. These incredible survivors have evolved to thrive in some of the world's harshest environments, storing water in their thick stems and reducing water loss through specialized spines instead of leaves.

Perfect for modern living, cacti require minimal care while providing maximum visual impact. Their unique shapes and occasional spectacular blooms make them living sculptures for your home or office.

Plant Specifications

Scientific Name: Cactaceae family
Plant Type: Succulent
Mature Size: 1 inch to 60 feet (species dependent)
Sun Exposure: Bright light to full sun
Soil Type: Well-draining, sandy or gritty soil
Bloom Time: Spring to summer (varies by species)
Hardiness Zones: 9-11 (indoors: any zone)

Care Instructions

Watering: Water thoroughly but infrequently. Allow soil to dry completely between waterings. Reduce watering in winter.

Light: Provide bright, indirect light indoors or full sun outdoors. Rotate occasionally for even growth.

Temperature: Most cacti prefer temperatures between 65-80Â°F (18-27Â°C) during growing season.

Soil: Use a cactus-specific potting mix or create your own with regular potting soil mixed with sand and perlite.

Fertilizing: Feed with diluted cactus fertilizer during growing season (spring and summer).
