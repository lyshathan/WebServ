Fern - Ancient Forest Beauty

Ferns are among the oldest plants on Earth, having thrived for over 360 million years. These remarkable plants reproduce through spores rather than seeds or flowers, and their delicate, feathery fronds bring an timeless elegance to any space.

Perfect for creating a lush, tropical atmosphere indoors, ferns thrive in humid conditions and filtered light, making them ideal for bathrooms, kitchens, or any space that needs a touch of natural beauty.

Plant Specifications

Scientific Name: Polypodiopsida class
Plant Type: Non-flowering vascular plant
Mature Size: 6 inches to 6 feet (species dependent)
Light Exposure: Bright, indirect light to partial shade
Soil Type: Well-draining, organic-rich soil
Humidity: High humidity (50-80%)
Hardiness Zones: 2-10 (varies by species)

Care Instructions

Watering: Keep soil consistently moist but not waterlogged. Use filtered or distilled water when possible as ferns are sensitive to chemicals.

Humidity: Maintain high humidity by misting regularly, using a humidifier, or placing on a pebble tray filled with water.

Light: Provide bright, indirect light. Avoid direct sunlight which can scorch the delicate fronds.

Fertilizing: Feed monthly during growing season with diluted, balanced liquid fertilizer.
