Rose - The Queen of Flowers

The rose is a woody perennial flowering plant of the genus Rosa, in the family Rosaceae. Known as the "Queen of Flowers," roses have been cultivated for over 5,000 years and are beloved worldwide for their beauty, fragrance, and symbolic meaning.

Roses are not just beautiful; they're also surprisingly resilient. With proper care, they can bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season, filling your garden with color and fragrance.

Plant Specifications

Scientific Name: Rosa spp.
Plant Type: Flowering Shrub
Mature Size: 1-20 feet tall, 1-15 feet wide
Sun Exposure: Full sun (6+ hours daily)
Soil Type: Well-draining, fertile soil
Bloom Time: Late spring through fall
Hardiness Zones: 3-11 (varies by variety)

Care Instructions

Watering: Water deeply at the base of the plant, avoiding wetting the leaves. Roses prefer consistent moisture but not waterlogged conditions.

Fertilizing: Feed with a balanced rose fertilizer in spring and mid-summer. Organic compost works wonderfully.

Pruning: Prune in late winter or early spring to remove dead wood and encourage new growth.

Pest Control: Watch for aphids, black spot, and powdery mildew. Good air circulation helps prevent fungal issues.
